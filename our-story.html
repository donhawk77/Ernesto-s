Our Story

Where French Artistry Meets Mexican Soul

The Beginning

A Dream Born in the Kitchen

In 1985, a young chef with French culinary training and a deep love for Mexican cuisine opened the doors to a small restaurant on Jackson Keller Road in San Antonio. His vision was simple but revolutionary: bring the precision and artistry of French cooking technique to the bold, soulful flavors of Mexican food.

What started as a neighborhood restaurant quickly became a destination. Word spread about the sauces that simmered for hours, the dressings made fresh each morning, the bread baked in-house — every detail crafted with a chef's obsession for quality.

Our Philosophy

From Scratch, Every Single Day

In an industry where shortcuts are the norm, Ernesto's stands apart. Every sauce — from our signature ranchera to our cream-based chipotle — is made from scratch daily. Every dressing. Every piece of bread. Even our tortilla chips are hand-cut and fried fresh.

This isn't a marketing claim. It's a sacred commitment that has defined our kitchen for four decades. Our guests can taste the difference, and it's why many have been coming back since the very beginning.

The Cuisine

French Technique, Mexican Heart

Ernesto's cuisine is unlike anything else in San Antonio. Classical French cooking methods — the mother sauces, the precise temperatures, the layered flavors — are applied to the ingredients and traditions of Mexican cooking.

The result? Dishes like our Baked Red Snapper stuffed with crabmeat, our Kabob Acapulco featuring shrimp, beef tenderloin, and lobster, and our enchiladas suizas with a sauce that takes three hours to perfect. Every plate tells the story of two culinary traditions united by one chef's passion.

Our Journey
